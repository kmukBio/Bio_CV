About

Decoding the spatial and molecular architecture of disease- from biological complexity into clinical impact

I am a PhD-trained bioinformatics and systems biologist, with over 10 years of experience leading multiomics data programs. My work focuses on integrating spatial, single-cell, and molecular datasets to generate scalable insights and enable AI-driven discovery.

Currently, as a Scientist at UC San Diego, I build analytical pipelines and translational strategies that accelerate scientific impact. I am deeply passionate about leveraging frontier AI technologies to convert complex biological signals into decision-grade outputs for advancement of human health- therapeutic prioritization and precision medicine.

Research

Focusing on the intersection of Frontier AI, Network Biology and Systems Medicine.

Oncology & Precision Medicine

Developing integrative -omics frameworks to identify key disease drivers in breast cancer (TNBC, HER2+) and colorectal cancer. My work utilizes spatio-molecular technolgies to define prognosis and understand drug resistance mechanisms.

Immunology & Infectious Disease

Mechanistic modeling of immune responses, specifically investigating the landscapes of COVID-19 and Dengue infections to identify biomarkers of severity and therapeutic targets.

Skeletal Muscle Pathophysiology

Systems-level analysis of muscle response to disease and treatment (e.g., Duchenne muscular dystrophy, botulinum neurotoxin), focusing on molecular structure and functional changes.
